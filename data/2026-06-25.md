Here, we provide experimental evidence that altermagnetic spin splitting in RuO2 is a strain-stabilized emergent state rather than an intrinsic bulk property. Angular-resolved spin-torque measurements reveal a symmetry-selected spin Hall response characteristic of altermagnetic spin splitting, which is strongest in the strained regime but progressively suppressed as the lattice relaxes toward the bulk limit. Complementary magnetic measurements further reveal enhanced coercivity and exchange-bias behavior exclusively in strained films, indicating the emergence of a strain-stabilized magnetic state. First-principles calculations reproduce the strain-dependent evolution of the Neel order and spin-split electronic structure, supporting the experimental observations. Together, these results establish altermagnetic spin splitting in RuO2 as a strain-stabilized emergent state and provide a unified explanation for the long-standing discrepancy between bulk and thin-film observations.
