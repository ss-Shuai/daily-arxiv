### [1] [Superconductivity mediated by nematic fluctuations -- the dispersion of collective modes](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05433)
*Kazi Ranjibul Islam,Andrey Chubukov*

Main category: cond-mat.supr-con

TL;DR: This paper analyzes collective excitation modes (phase and amplitude modes) in an unconventional s-wave superconductor mediated by long-range nematic fluctuations, where the superconducting gap is highly anisotropic and vanishes on four Fermi surface arcs. The pair susceptibility spectral function is computed and compared to conventional BCS superconductors.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation stems from experimental observations of exotic superconductivity in materials like Sr2RuO4 and iron-based superconductors, where pairing is mediated by nematic fluctuations rather than phonons. These systems exhibit highly anisotropic gaps that vanish on parts of the Fermi surface, raising questions about how collective modes behave in such unconventional settings compared to standard BCS theory.

Method: The authors derive the pair susceptibility χ(q,Ω) at finite momentum q and frequency Ω in the superconducting state. They analyze the spectral function Imχ(q,Ω) and its pole structure, distinguishing between transverse (phase) and longitudinal (amplitude) channels, and compare these results with conventional s-wave BCS superconductors.

Result: They find that the analytic structure of pair susceptibility in both phase and amplitude channels is qualitatively distinct from conventional BCS superconductors. This gives rise to highly unconventional dispersions of phase and amplitude collective modes, unlike the standard Anderson-Higgs mode or Leggett mode expected in BCS theory.

Conclusion: The distinct gap structure (vanishing on four arcs of the Fermi surface) fundamentally changes the collective mode spectrum, producing exotic dispersion relations not described by conventional BCS theory. This provides a framework for understanding experiments probing collective excitations in nematic-mediated superconductors.

Abstract: We analyze the spectrum of collective modes in a superconductor in which pairing is mediated by long-range nematic fluctuations. Previous experimental and theoretical studies have found that the superconducting gap in such a system is highly anisotropic and, at any finite $T<T_c$, vanishes on four arcs of the Fermi surface, even when the pairing symmetry is $s$ wave ($s^{+-}$ between hole and electron pockets). We derive the expression for the pair susceptibility $χ(\mathbf{q},Ω)$ at finite momentum $\mathbf{q}$ and frequency $Ω$ deep in the superconducting phase. We analyze the spectral function, $\operatorname{Im}χ(\mathbf{q},Ω)$, and its pole structure in the transverse (phase) and longitudinal (amplitude) channels, and compare the results with those of a conventional $s$-wave superconductor. We find that the analytic structure of the pair susceptibility in both channels is qualitatively distinct from that in a BCS superconductor. This gives rise to a highly unconventional dispersion of phase and amplitude collective modes.
